Hojicha soft serve is roasted green tea in frozen form: toasty, a little nutty,
with the same low-bitterness profile that makes hojicha easy to drink. The roast
survives freezing better than you'd expect, so good versions taste clearly of
tea, not just brown vanilla.

## Why it's rare here

Matcha soft serve is everywhere in San Francisco. Hojicha is the harder one to
find. Most shops that scoop Japanese-tea soft serve lead with matcha, and hojicha
shows up as the occasional swirl or seasonal flavor, if at all.

## What good looks like

- Real roasted-tea flavor, not just sweet and beige.
- Smooth, not icy.
- Restraint on the sugar, so the toastiness comes through.
